The Answer Company
A comprehensive site for custom print and promotional products

TheAnswerCompany.com is a custom wordpress site that strikes a balance between design and function. Pages maintain a logical, visual order while coming together to still boast a comprehensive catalog with over 40 pages. While I did not develop the original site, I have since taken the lead on the project, maintaining, expanding, and improving the site for the past 8 months. You can check out the site over at TheAnswerCompany.com

Fine Tuning

In general, website development can broadly be broken down into two phases: the initial construction and the subsequent maintenance and scaling that follows. As the primary developer on the site, I often receive requests to add new elements, adjust styling, or update the content.

Two examples of a request for minor adjustments from the designer. On the left, a very subtle adjustment of padding to center align an element. On the right, a more detailed spec sheet for an existing element.

A slightly more comprehensive example that involves changing a header layout and incorporating some buttons. Some elements are disregarded altogether.

While generally as simple as it sounds, maintaining responsiveness with new elements can be the bulk of the work.

A closer look at the different elements used for button states for the top nav bar.

Scaling Up

In addition to the ever going 'fine tuning' of the site, there are larger projects that require the creation of new elements, pages, and features.

The evolution of a mail tout element. An entirely new element is first created and then adjusted according to the designer's preferences.

A single main image featured on landing and hub pages was upgraded to a rotating slider, displaying more examples of what the company has to offer. This upgrade also came with an update in code. PHP allowed for consolidating markup into functions that could easily be added to each page. This also allowed the original single-image format to be quickly reverted to (as it is for some pages)

A more comprehensive addition to the site involved adding a new 'hub' element to each section, consolidating much of the content. Another example of element specifications used for responsive design is depicted on the right.

A side by side comparison of the old and new landing page. Element arrangement of the side bar and the addition of a button at the bottom of the hub are also implemented.

Aside from my own website, I hadn't really appreciated the second phase of website building. I would always get requests from old clients for advice or an occasional tweak, but I hadn't collaborated with a web designer on a project to project basis like I did with TheAnswerCompany.com. This communication along with the slower pace of maintaining and expanding a site over time was a good step in elevating my experience as a developer.
